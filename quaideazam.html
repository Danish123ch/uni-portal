About Quaid E Azam University:

Quaid-e-Azam University (QAU), established in 1967 in Islamabad, is one of Pakistan’s premier public research universities. Named after the founder of Pakistan, Muhammad Ali Jinnah (Quaid-e-Azam), it has rapidly evolved into a leading institution for higher education and research in the country. The university is located in the picturesque Margalla Hills, providing a serene environment for academic pursuits. QAU offers a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across various disciplines, including Natural Sciences, Social Sciences, and Humanities. It is renowned for its strong emphasis on research and innovation, consistently ranking among the top universities in Pakistan and gaining recognition in global university rankings for its academic excellence and impactful research contributions.

ABOUT VC:

Prof. Dr. Niaz Ahmad Akhtar is the current Vice Chancellor of Quaid-e-Azam University (QAU), Islamabad, having assumed office in January 2025. A distinguished academic and administrator, he holds a PhD in Mechanical Engineering and has previously served as the Vice Chancellor of the University of Sialkot and the University of Engineering and Technology (UET) Lahore. Dr. Akhtar is renowned for his commitment to academic excellence, research innovation, and fostering a culture of integrity and inclusivity within higher education institutions. His leadership at QAU focuses on enhancing research output, promoting interdisciplinary collaboration, and ensuring that the university continues to produce graduates who are well-equipped to contribute to Pakistan's development and global competitiveness.
